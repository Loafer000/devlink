Resume Writing Tips

Use Action Verbs

Start bullet points with strong verbs: Led, Developed, Increased, Managed, Created

Quantify Achievements

Include numbers: "Increased sales by 45%" instead of "Increased sales"

Keep It Concise

Aim for 1-2 pages. Focus on relevant experience from the last 10-15 years

Tailor for Each Job

Customize your resume using keywords from the job description

Proofread Carefully

Check for typos, grammar errors, and formatting consistency

Resume Sections Explained

Contact Information

Include your full name, phone number, email, LinkedIn profile, and location (city/state)

Tip: Use a professional email address

Professional Summary

2-3 sentences highlighting your experience, skills, and career goals

Tip: Tailor this section for each application

Work Experience

List jobs in reverse chronological order with achievements and responsibilities

Tip: Focus on accomplishments, not just duties

Education

Include degree, institution, graduation year, and relevant coursework or honors

Tip: Recent grads can place this before experience

Skills

List technical and soft skills relevant to the position

Tip: Match skills to job requirements

Additional Sections

Certifications, languages, volunteer work, publications, or projects

Tip: Only include if relevant to the role
